## Portfolio Audience — Who You're Writing For

The primary audience for this portfolio is **hiring managers recruiting for PMM (Product Marketing Manager) and Content Marketing roles**.

Every piece of copy — skill names, project descriptions, impact statements, headlines — must be written with this reader in mind.

### Rules for all content:
- **Use job-description vocabulary** — terms hiring managers ctrl+F for: "Competitive Intelligence" not "Competitor Analysis", "Demand Generation" not "Lead Generation", "Social Media Growth" not just "Social Media", "Product Launches" not "Launch Management"
- **Lead with outcomes that matter to each role** — revenue and conversion metrics for PMM readers; audience growth and engagement for content readers
- **Signal the role type early** — a PMM reader should see GTM/positioning/launch in the first line; a content reader should see strategy/production/growth
- **Show seniority, not just activity** — "owns the content pipeline end-to-end" beats "creates content"; "drove ₹50L+ monthly revenue" beats "launched a program"
- **Never write generic marketing copy** — every sentence should feel like it was written for someone who knows the craft

## Resume Sync Protocol

The resumes in `content/resumes/` are the **source of truth**. There are two versions targeting different roles:

- **`product_marketing.tex`** — Targets product marketing / GTM / monetisation roles. Emphasises customer insights → revenue, A/B testing, pricing, funnel metrics.
- **`content_marketing.tex`** — Targets content / social media / brand roles. Emphasises on-camera, content production, audience growth, engagement rates.

### When adding or updating projects from a resume:
1. Read the relevant resume for the most current metrics and framing
2. Update `PRIYANKA_PERSONA.md` (this file) with any new numbers or roles
3. Update the relevant `content/projects/[slug]/brief.md` with accurate numbers
4. If a **new role or project** appears in the resume that has no brief.md:
   - Flag it to Priyanka: *"X is in your resume but not in the portfolio — should I add it?"*
   - Ask for: project summary, images/proof of work, Canva/presentation links, key metrics
   - Once confirmed, create the brief.md and update this file
5. **Never fabricate metrics** — only use numbers explicitly stated in the resumes

### Metrics reconciliation (when the two resumes differ):
- For social channel growth (Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn): use the **content_marketing resume** numbers (those are the content-focused metrics)
- For revenue, leads, enrollments, conversion rates: use the **product_marketing resume** numbers
- When in doubt, use the higher/more recent number and flag the discrepancy to Priyanka
